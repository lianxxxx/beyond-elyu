# beyond elyu

> La Union is not overrated. Look closer.

**Register:** brand

A single-page editorial site about **La Union**, the surf coast on the
northwest of Luzon. The whole site exists to make one argument: that the place
people think they already know from a crowded weekend in San Juan is only the
sliver they saw, and the rest is worth slowing down for. The design *is* the
product. A visitor's impression, the feeling that they underestimated the
place, is the thing being made.

This is a vibe-coded passion project, not a commercial product. It can take
risks a client site couldn't.

## Users

- **The day-tripper who thinks they've "done" La Union.** Came for Urbiztondo,
  surfed, ate, left. The site's whole job is to make this person pause. They
  are the antagonist the copy argues with.
- **The slow traveler / second-time visitor.** Open to the idea that there's
  more. Wants the towns, the history, the food, the falls, not a checklist.
- **People who love good web design.** This is a portfolio-grade build; some
  visitors come for the craft as much as the place.

## Product purpose

Persuade, then guide. The page is a single argument delivered as a scroll:
hero (the claim) → intro (the framing) → the map (the territory) → cities and
municipalities (the evidence: each town's short history and what it's known
for) → a persuasive close (the turn) → footer. By the end, "overrated" should
feel like a misunderstanding the visitor used to hold.

## Brand voice and tone

- **Quietly contrarian.** It argues, but never shouts. Confidence, not hype.
- **Warm and specific.** Place names, local detail, real history. The opposite
  of generic travel-brochure adjectives.
- **A second look, literally.** The voice rewards re-reading; lines turn on a
  closer reading the way the place turns on a closer visit.
- Lowercase wordmark ("elyu"), sentence-case copy, full La Union place names.

## Strategic principles

- **The Second Look** is the north star. Detail and meaning emerge on a longer
  gaze: motion reveals as you scroll, hierarchy reveals on a second read,
  the argument lands on reflection. Never everything-at-once.
- **One argument, one page.** Every section advances "look closer." If a
  section doesn't move that argument, it doesn't belong.
- **Place over polish.** Real La Union, real towns, real photos. Imagery is
  required, not optional; this is a travel brief and zero images is a bug.
- **Earned motion.** Choreographed entrances and scroll reveals, but always
  calm: ease-out, no bounce, nothing that distracts from the place.

## Anti-references (what this is NOT)

- **Not the cream-and-gold "luxury travel" template.** No beige-and-muted
  affectation, no serif-italic-drop-cap editorial-magazine cosplay. The palette
  is its own warm coastal system, not a borrowed luxury one.
- **Not borrowed type.** No reflex display serif (Playfair / Cormorant /
  Fraunces). The voice is Clash Display + Geist, chosen, not defaulted.
- **Not the SaaS landing-page shape.** No hero-metric template (big number +
  small label + stat row), no identical icon-heading-text card grids, no
  centered icon-title-subtitle stack.
- **Not a checklist / listicle.** "Top 10 things to do in La Union" is exactly
  the day-tripper energy the site argues against.
- **Not glassmorphism-by-default.** The hero's translucent capsules are a
  deliberate over-photo treatment, not a license to glass every surface.
